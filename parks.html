Fair questions

What owners ask us

I'm not planning to sell. Is this still for me?
Yes — most owners who call us aren't selling. A written valuation is simply good information to own: for estate planning, for family conversations, for refinancing, or for judging the next unsolicited offer that lands in your mailbox. There's no follow-up campaign and no pressure. When you want to talk again, you call us.
What does it cost? Where's the catch?
Nothing, and there isn't one. We're paid a competitive commission only if you one day choose to sell and we represent you — the same way representation normally works. The free valuation is how we introduce ourselves. If we do a sloppy job on it, you'll know exactly what we're worth.
Will my residents or manager find out?
No. We never contact your residents, your staff, or your manager. We never disclose that your park was discussed, valued, or offered. If you later decide to sell, marketing is done quietly to verified buyers under confidentiality — not with a sign at your entrance.
Is this an appraisal?
No — and we're careful about the difference. What we provide is a documented market valuation — a comparative market analysis prepared by a licensed Nevada real estate professional, based on income, comparable sales, and current buyer demand. If you ever need a certified appraisal (for a lender or court, for example), we'll tell you so and point you to one.
How do I know the buyers are real?
We follow the national and regional groups acquiring Nevada parks — what they buy, at what size, and in which counties. Your park is only ever approached to buyers whose criteria it actually fits, and only with your go-ahead. We don't mass-market your property to strangers.
